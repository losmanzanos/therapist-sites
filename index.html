About the studio

Tech, made with
actual care.

Most therapists didn't go into private practice to wrestle with website builders on the weekend. You chose this work to help people — and your online presence should reflect the care and craft you bring to every session.

I'm Chad, a Denver-based developer whose wife runs her own group practice. I've built her site. I know this world — the directories, the niche specialties, the way clients actually find you, the language that lands and the language that doesn't.

Every site I build is hand-coded from scratch. No themes. No page builders. No two practices ever get the same template, because no two practices are the same. What you get is a small, fast, beautifully made website that's yours — your domain, your hosting, your code, your name on everything.

And unlike subscription platforms, there are no ongoing fees that creep up every year. You pay once, own everything outright, and never have to worry about a company raising rates — or disappearing — and taking your site with it.

How this works

Considered,
not complicated.

A four-step process that keeps things moving without ever leaving you wondering what comes next. No project management software, no onboarding portals. Just clear communication and consistent progress.

i.

The first call

Thirty minutes, free, no pitch deck. You tell me about your practice and the kind of clients you want to reach. I tell you honestly whether I'm the right person for the project.

ii.

Design & build

I design and code your site from scratch, sharing live previews along the way. We iterate on look, voice, and structure together. Usually three to five weeks, start to finish.

iii.

Review & refine

You review the site, request changes, and we fine-tune until everything feels right. I'm responsive and unhurried — this isn't an assembly line.

iv.

Launch & after

Your site goes live. I'm here for post-launch questions for the first 60 days, available on retainer or hourly after that. You're never left on your own.
